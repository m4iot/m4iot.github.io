Program of the Federated M4IoT/MECC/DFSD/MISE Workshop

This is the program of the federated M4IoT/MECC/DFSD/MISE workshop.

Monday, December 9th 2019

  • 9:00 am - Introduction and keynote by Nalini Venkatasubramanian
    Topic: Enabling Trustworthy Community Infrastructures with IoT
    Abstract: T.b.d.
  • 9:50 am - Session: Fog Programming
    • A Survey on Fog Programming: Concepts, State-of-the-Art, and Research Challenges (Dapeng Lan, Amir Taherkordi, Frank Eliassen and Geir Horn) DFSD
    • ProgCEP: A Programming Model for Complex Event Processing over Fog Infrastructure (Manisha Luthra and Boris Koldehofe) DFSD
  • 10:30 am - Coffee break
  • 11:00 am - Session: Edge Computing
    • Container Deployment Strategy for Edge Networking (Walter Wong, Aleksandr Zavodovski, Pengyuan Zhou and Jussi Kangasharju) MECC
    • Tango of Edge and Cloud Execution for Reliability (Shikhar Suryavansh, Chandan Bothra, Mung Chiang, Chunyi Peng and Saurabh Bagchi) MECC
    • Towards distributed, fair, and deadline-driven resource allocation for Cloudlets (Stratos Dimopoulos, Chandra Krintz and Rich Wolski) MECC
  • 12:00 pm - Lunch break
  • 1:30 pm - Session: Middleware
    • SORRIR: A Resilient Self-organizing Middleware for IoT Applications (Jörg Domaschka, Christian Berger, Hans P. Reiser, Philipp Eichhammer, Frank Griesinger, Jakob Pietron, Matthias Tichy, Franz J. Hauck, Gerhard Habiger) M4IoT, short paper
    • Semantic Description of Artificial DNA for an Organic Computing Middleware Architecture (Uwe Brinkschulte, Mathias Pacher) MISE
    • Towards a QoS-aware Cyber Physical Networking Middleware Architecture (Melanie Brinkschulte, Christian Becker, Christian Krupitzer) MISE
  • 2:30 pm - Session: Middleware
    • Experience Report: Enhancing Transparency of MQTT Brokers For IoT Applications Through Provenance Streams (Milan Markovic and Peter Edwards) M4IoT, short paper
    • Automatic Translation of Spatio-Temporal Logics to Streaming-Based Monitoring Applications for IoT-Equipped Autonomous Agents (Luca Bortolussi, Vincenzo Gulisano, Eric Medvet, Dimitrios Palyvos-Giannas) M4IoT
  • 3:00 pm - Coffee break
  • 3:30 pm - Session: Software Engineering for IoT
    • Untangling the physical-digital knot when designing advanced IoT ecosystems (Ilse Bohé, Michiel Willocx, Vincent Naessens) M4IoT
    • A Development Environment for FIWARE-based Internet of Things Applications (Lucas Dantas, Everton Cavalcante, Thais Batista) M4IoT
  • 4:10 pm - Discussion and Wrap-Up
